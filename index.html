Bio

I am a PhD candidate at Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University, in the Department of Technology & Operations Management.  I conduct research on human-AI interaction.

Previous I worked as a commodities trader in Europe, the US, and Asia.

Research

I am broadly interested in how humans make decisions against a certain causal structure or representation of the world.  Recently, the adoption of statistical learning algorithms has introduced a novel causal context for studying the human learning-cum-decision-making process.  My research explores how statistical learning algorithms interact with human agency, human causal reasoning, organizational information-processing, and organizational decision-making.

Interests

I have interests in philosophy, literature and cinema.  My favorite writers are Georg Büchner and Herman Melville.  My favorite poets are Hölderlin and Celan.  I am currently studying Sanskrit and reading The Bhagavad Gita.

Lately, I've become an aficionado of Tamil, Malayalam, and Kannada cinemas.  Some recent films I enjoyed watching include Rocky, Saani Kaayidham, Joji, and Garuda Gamana Vrishabha Vahana.

I've been reading Statistical Inference as Severe Testing, Causation with a Human Face, Evolutionary Processes and Organizational Adaptation, and Buddhist Phenomenology with enthusiasm.
